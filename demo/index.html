Welcome

Many of us are obsessed with food and behavioural processes such as restricting, overeating, bingeing and purging and/or constantly thinking about food and weight loss – and have no idea these could be symptoms of addiction.

I was labeled as ‘having an eating disorder’ for 30+ years. I tried many treatment programs – all emphasized ‘moderation of all foods’ - including residential in-patient and outpatient programs, psychotherapy and psychiatry (anti-depressants and other medications).

When I saw my problem was addiction – I FINALLY found a recovery protocol that worked.

And if you have the disease of addiction – I would be privileged to work with you!

Addicts living in recovery are accomplished, creative, intuitive and intelligent – and often healthier and wealthier than ‘normal’ people – once we learn how addiction works and how to manage it.

Is This Your Experience?

Millions of people are struggling with mood, weight, eating and sleep issues. Many have tried therapy, medication, surgery and different diets to feel better.
Yet nothing seems to last.

You may need to be screened and professionally assessed for food addiction, if any of the following apply to you:

  • You are reactive or defensive at the suggestion of being food addicted
  • You gain weight even when following nutrition guidelines
  • You are obsessed with food, eating, dieting, body image – the thoughts never seem to stop
  • You cannot stop thinking about eating and food even when you have just eaten
  • You feel guilt or shame regarding your food and eating thoughts and behaviours and lie about what and how you eat
  • You have a pattern of eating/restricting/bingeing and/or using alcohol/drugs to cope
  • You think life would not be worth living without your favorite foods
  • You have been diagnosed as ‘eating disordered’, told there are “no bad foods” and “to eat everything in moderation” and it just doesn't work

Don’t despair - there is light at the end of the tunnel! A food addiction is not a death sentence - instead, you may find its a relief to know it is not your fault. And you can learn how to put it into remission and live a life of freedom you never thought possible.

What is SUGAR®

Are you addicted to certain foods and/or eating behaviours?

SUGAR® is a structured evaluation tool that examines a client’s relationship with sugar/flour/food and related consequences. Prior to the development of SUGAR® there was no diagnostic instrument available for “food addiction”, other than screening tools including UNCOPE-S, Yale scale, and various self-assessment questionnaires.

SUGAR® is based on the diagnostic criteria for harmful use, pathological use and addiction to mind-altering (psychoactive) drugs as described in the ICD-10 (International Classification of Diseases) from WHO. Recent research shows sugar/flour, and highly processed foods can act as psychoactive drugs on peoples’ reward system, especially those with a predisposition towards addiction.

SUGAR® is a tool for professionals who work with people who may have developed problematic or chemical dependence (addiction) to sugar/flour/food.

SUGAR® results determine whether a client has “harmful use” or “pathological use” (addiction) to flour/sugar/food and the addiction development curve will show a client’s relationship with sugar/flour/food in chronological order. It is a strong motivational tool and clinically proven to increase client’s compliance with treatment protocol.

The Process

Jayn engages her clients in a thorough Pre-Assessment Evaluation (PAE) process to uncover their unique physical, mental, emotional and behavioural challenges and strengths. If warranted, the next step is a SUGAR® assessment interview.

Finally, based on the PAE and SUGAR® results, Jayn will recommend a practical treatment recovery plan, including: an initial abstinent food plan, educational material/information, recommendations for specific laboratory tests and/or medical follow up, recommendations for exercise and sleep, and appropriate community-based support.
